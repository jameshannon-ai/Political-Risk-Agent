# Political Risk Brief
## Cyber Business Interruption Engine: Operational Resilience and Insurance Exposure for UK Retail / Critical Services

## 1. Decision Recommendation

| Item | Assessment |
| --- | --- |
| Recommended stance | Activate resilience controls: incident response readiness, notification review, insurance claim preparation, manual contingency planning and supplier/MSP escalation. |
| Current decision | Do not treat cyber disruption as a technical IT issue only. The illustrative model shows expected recovery exceeding tolerable downtime, creating business interruption exposure. |
| Primary operational trigger | Expected recovery time exceeds maximum tolerable downtime. |
| Primary regulatory trigger | Personal data breach or sector notification threshold may require ICO / regulator / customer notification review. |
| Primary insurance trigger | Policy notice condition, waiting period or material business interruption threshold may activate the claim process. |
| Manual contingency trigger | Core digital channel unavailable while manual process can preserve critical service or customer commitments. |
| Supplier/MSP trigger | Third-party technology, cloud, payment, fulfilment or managed-service failure blocks recovery. |

## 2. Scope and Specificity

This is a client-type screen for a UK customer-facing operator with material digital trading, payment, fulfilment, customer-service or essential-service dependency. Retail is the base showcase scenario; critical-services duties may be stricter and require sector-specific legal/regulatory review.

The output focuses on business interruption, operational resilience, regulatory notification, cyber insurance and recovery decisions. It does not provide technical cybersecurity advice.

## 6. Business Interruption Model

The business interruption exposure model is illustrative: business interruption exposure = outage duration x daily revenue at risk.

The resilience gap model is illustrative: resilience gap = maximum tolerable downtime - expected recovery time.

| Input | Value | Label | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Expected outage | 5 days | illustrative | Estimates downtime exposure |
| Maximum tolerable downtime | 2 days | illustrative / company-provided when available | Tests resilience tolerance |
| Resilience gap | -3 days | derived | Shows expected recovery exceeds tolerance |
| Daily revenue at risk | £2.0m | illustrative | Estimates gross revenue disruption |
| Gross revenue disruption | £10.0m | derived | Outage duration x daily revenue at risk |
| Insurance waiting period | 24 hours | illustrative | Tests claim timing and uninsured loss period |
| Regulatory notification trigger | source-supported where applicable | source-supported | Tests ICO / regulator notification review |
| Customer harm / service disruption severity | high | illustrative | Supports notification, prioritisation and customer-impact review |

The resilience gap is negative where expected recovery takes longer than maximum tolerable downtime. In this illustrative case, a negative three-day resilience gap means expected recovery takes longer than the organisation can tolerate, turning cyber disruption into a business-continuity, notification and insurance-response issue rather than a purely technical IT problem.

## 11. Mitigation Options

| Option | Use when | Decision owner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Continue normal operations | No compromise indicator, no notification trigger and recovery capability remains inside tolerance. | COO / Risk Manager |
| Activate incident response | Outage or compromise indicators exceed normal operational tolerance. | Incident response lead |
| Notify regulator / affected customers | Personal data breach or statutory trigger appears. | Legal / Compliance / Risk |
| Trigger cyber insurance claim process | Waiting period, notice condition or material interruption threshold may be met. | CFO / Risk Manager / Broker |
| Switch to manual contingency | Expected recovery exceeds maximum tolerable downtime and manual operations are viable. | COO / Head of Operations |
| Escalate supplier / MSP dependency | Third-party service failure blocks recovery. | COO / Procurement / Technology |
| Pause exposed digital operations | Continuing activity worsens customer harm, data exposure or recovery risk. | COO / Legal / Incident response lead |
| Increase resilience investment | Expected recovery exceeds tolerable downtime or testing shows recovery weakness. | Board / CFO / COO |

## 19. Methodology and Review Controls

This is a client-type cyber business interruption exposure screen, not technical cybersecurity advice, legal advice or an insurance coverage determination.

Company-specific use requires:
- affected systems and process map
- daily revenue by channel / service
- RTO / RPO
- backup and restore capability
- cyber insurance policy wording
